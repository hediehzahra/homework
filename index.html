Saturn



History

Saturn took shape when the rest of the solar system formed about 4.5 billion years ago when gravity pulled swirling gas and dust in to become this gas giant.
About 4 billion years ago, Saturn settled into its current position in the outer solar system, where it is the sixth planet from the Sun.



Planet Saturn Overview

Saturn is the sixth planet from the sun and the second largest planet in our solar system. Adorned with a dazzling system of icy rings,Saturn is unique among the planets.
It is not the only planet to have rings,but none are as spectacular or as complex as Saturn`s. Like fellow gas giant Jupiter,Saturn is a massive ball made mostly of
hydrogen and helium. The farthest planet from Earth discovered by the unaided human eye ,Saturn has been known since ancient times. The planet is named for
the Roman god of agriculture and wealth, who was also the father of Jupiter.
